Revenue, sentiment, and
what to do about it.

The data suggests a common gacha pattern: a large launch peak, a high 2024 plateau, and a sustained decline through 2025–26, with temporary recoveries around anniversaries and collaborations. Player sentiment moved on a separate track — driven less by generosity than by perceived roster balance and story pacing. This brief pairs each observation with a candidate cause and an operational response.

Data provenance — read before citing

  • Mobile only. Every figure is an iOS + Google Play estimate. It excludes PC (HoYoverse launcher / Epic), PS5 (live since Oct 2023), and Chinese third-party Android stores. Naavik modelled PC alone at ~$272.7M through Aug 2023 [R6], so all-platform totals are higher than shown.
  • Third-party estimates, not official. HoYoverse discloses no revenue. Trackers differ on method (gross vs. net, calendar vs. rolling 30-day), which is why some same-period figures disagree; both are shown where they do.
  • No per-patch figures exist. Finest verifiable granularity is monthly. A 42-day patch spans ~1.4 months across two banner phases, so revenue is mapped to the month it falls in — not attributed per patch.
  • Sentiment is a directional read. Tags below are editorial, evidence-linked where sources exist. Quantified per-platform scoring would require a tracked index (defined in the KPI section), not a manual estimate per patch.

Analysis & response

Four eras — observation, cause, action

Each era pairs the pattern with a candidate cause and a proposed operational response. Causes not directly evidenced by the data are marked as hypotheses.

1.xApr–Dec 2023

Launch and the plateau baseline

Revenue peaked at launch (~$245M gross in 30 days; $500M by month three) and normalised into a high baseline. Goodwill was established early, reinforced by the v1.6 free 5★ Dr. Ratio — setting the perception of HSR as generous relative to Genshin.

$132.3M (30d) → $500M (25 Jul) → free-5★ goodwill

Observation
Content-supply and endgame-cadence comments appeared even during a strong revenue period.
Candidate cause
Novelty-driven launch spend normalising; engaged players clearing content faster than it ships.
Recommendation
  • Treat permanent progression / endgame supply as an early retention lever, not a late-game fix.
  • Bank launch goodwill as a baseline expectation to protect, not a one-off.
2.xFeb–Dec 2024

Penacony — the revenue plateau

2024 was the strongest full year ($870.9M mobile, above Genshin). The data associates the plateau with story reception and marquee banners: Acheron (2.1) coincides with the ~$92.47M March reading; Firefly (2.3) was a high-demand banner. In parallel, community discussion of powercreep shifted from occasional to recurring.

$92.47M reading · $870.9M year · DAU 3.33M

Observation
Revenue strength and powercreep concern rose together; each marquee unit raised the effective damage ceiling.
Candidate cause
Monetisation leaning on escalating banner power; 4★ viability (e.g. Gallagher) buffering budget players, but unevenly.
Recommendation
  • Track a powercreep-perception signal alongside banner revenue, so the two are read together.
  • Protect a reliable 4★ / budget option per arc to keep non-payer teams viable.
  • Use the story-driven plateau to test whether narrative peaks can carry revenue without a power jump.
3.xJan–Dec 2025

Amphoreus — spikes on a declining baseline

The clearest divergence in the dataset. v3.0 drew documented feedback on lore density and puzzle complexity, prompting a public developer response on 5 Feb 2025 that committed to skip options and pacing changes [R12][R13]. Alongside, roster economics tightened: Amphoreus shipped no new 4★ units, increasing premium-team dependence [R16]. Yet the year's two largest readings came from the 2nd anniversary (3.2, ~$71.1M) and the Fate collaboration (3.4, $78.8M).

$71.1M anniv. · $78.8M collab · ~$2B lifetime

Observation
Revenue spiked on events while baseline sentiment softened — the two signals point in opposite directions.
Candidate cause
Arc-transition onboarding friction; Remembrance-path powercreep; absence of budget units forcing full premium teams. Hypothesis: endgame (MoC) tuning assumed access to new premium units.
Recommendation
  • Reinstate a per-arc 4★ cadence to lower forced premium-team dependence.
  • Keep the v3.0 skip-option response as a precedent; make responsive pacing standard, not reactive.
  • Re-examine endgame difficulty assumptions so clears don't require the newest banner.
  • Increase Developer Radio cadence around arc transitions, when friction is highest.
4.xFeb–Jul 2026

Sustained decline, collab-dependent recovery

Monthly readings step down ($58.1M → $38.8M → $28.4M) with MAU already ~19.4M by Dec 2025 — consistent with a maturing live-service title past its spending peak. The counter-move to date is IP leverage: extending the Fate collaboration into 4.4, echoing the 3.4 pattern of using a collaboration to lift a falling baseline.

$58.1M → $38.8M → $28.4M (Apr–Jun ’26)

Observation
Revenue recovery is increasingly concentrated in collaboration windows rather than core content.
Candidate cause
DAU contraction; completion of long arcs; weaker perceived banner pull-value. Hypotheses (not verifiable here): higher acquisition cost and stronger competition for spend.
Recommendation
  • Strengthen permanent progression rewards to defend the baseline between events.
  • Reduce structural reliance on collabs by improving core-content pull-value.
  • Prioritise player-confidence investments (roster balance, communication) over short-term spike engineering.

Evidence, not vibes

Sentiment: method & a worked case

The register's Positive / Mixed / Friction tags are directional editorial reads. A production process would score a multi-platform, volume-weighted index per patch (see KPIs). Below is one fully-sourced case showing the structure that index would formalise.

v3.0Amphoreus launch15 Jan 2025
Channels read
Reddit r/HonkaiStarRailHoYoLABNGA (CN)BilibiliTwitter/X
Main complaints
Excessive lore exposition early in the arc; over-complex exploration puzzles gating side content; overall pacing. [R12][R13]
Main praise
Region art direction and world design; music; later, the Phainon storyline received positive critical reception. [R42]
Developer response
"Developer Radio | 3.0 Amphoreus: Special Edition," 5 Feb 2025 — acknowledged feedback and committed to skip options and pacing optimisation. [R12]
Resolution
Skip / pacing adjustments folded into subsequent versions; treated here as evidence of a functioning feedback loop, and a reusable response template.
Method note
A scored index would weight by post volume and platform, translate CN channels, and separate story sentiment from balance sentiment — the two move independently and should not be collapsed into one tag.

Synthesis

Takeaways for live operations

01

Anniversary patches reliably generate revenue spikes. Plan them as calendar anchors and set the baseline expectation accordingly, rather than reading each spike as new momentum.

02

Sentiment increasingly depends on perceived long-term roster balance rather than launch-day generosity. Powercreep management is a community-health lever, not only a design one.

03

Revenue resilience is leaning more on collaboration events than core content — a concentration risk worth monitoring, since it makes the baseline harder to defend between collabs.

04

Story quality remains a goodwill driver that appears largely independent of monetisation. It is a low-cost lever for sentiment that does not require a power jump.

05

Content planning should weight sustainable player confidence alongside short-term revenue opportunities. The 3.0 feedback loop is a repeatable model for responding when friction appears.
